# 1

Research shows that older adults are significantly more likely to believe and share misinformation compared to younger generations. One of the key reasons behind this is confirmation bias.

Confirmation bias is the psychological tendency to seek out, favour, and remember information that confirms what we already believe — while ignoring evidence that contradicts it. In today's digital landscape, where AI algorithms continuously feed us content tailored to our existing preferences, this bias is silently amplified without us even noticing.

# 2

We created an interactive web simulation designed to help older adults better recognise and respond to fake news.

Rather than simply reading about AI and confirmation bias, this experience is designed to be felt — through hands-on interactions that make these invisible mechanisms visible.
